PolliQuad

A Solution to the Global Pollination Crisis Through the Use of Quadcopters

Date

March 2016

Category

Research

Mentor

Bekk Blando

[insert email]

Status

Incomplete

Pollination is needed for about three-quarters of global food crops. The decline of honeybee colonies due to disease and pesticides has prompted serious concern. The results of poor pollination can be seen in crop yields across the world. Corn has proven to be a good indicator for pollination decline being an issue. Experiments conducted, and observations made on corn have seen a significant decline in crop quality and quantity. If the trend of decreasing pollinator populations is not interrupted and stopped, this would result in decreasing food production. Eventually food production might even stop altogether. In this research project, I attempted to develop a system using the Crazyflie 2.0 quadcopter by BitCraze to pollinate a tree.
