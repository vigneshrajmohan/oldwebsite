The Ability of Moringa Oleifera to Remove Metal-Based Contaminants from Water

Date

August 2017

Category

Research

Mentor

Dr. Kunlun Hong

kunlun7@gmail.com

Usable water is a vital and very limited resource in our society. Obtaining such water through purification is of imminent importance. One of the main challenges in water purification is the ability to get the tools and purification devices to the people in need efficiently and affordably. This project describes using the abundant Moringa oleifera (abbrev. Moringa) seed to coagulate contaminants in non-potable water. We demonstrate that the Moringa seed is a reasonably effective way to remove metal ion contaminants from water, especially for underdeveloped/developing areas where it is readily available. We believe our results offer a new avenue of using nature existed materials to purify water and have the potential to mitigate water crisis in the developing world.
